# Quality Checklist

Run this checklist before finalizing any academic writing output.

## Meaning Preservation

- Does the revised text preserve the original technical meaning?
- Are original claims, conditions, limitations, and uncertainty retained?
- Has any conclusion been strengthened without evidence?

## Academic Style

- Is the tone formal, objective, and precise?
- Are colloquial expressions removed?
- Are subjective, promotional, or exaggerated phrases avoided?
- Are transitions and logical relations clear?

## Technical Accuracy

- Are technical terms consistent?
- Are methods, datasets, metrics, and results accurately preserved?
- Are equations, abbreviations, and symbols handled consistently?
- Are field-specific terms appropriate?

## Evidence and Citation Safety

- No fake references.
- No fake author names or publication years.
- No fake datasets.
- No fake numerical results.
- No unsupported superiority, clinical, or deployment claims.
- Missing information is marked with placeholders or questions.

## Translation Quality

For CN to EN:

- Avoid literal translation.
- Preserve logical relations.
- Use natural academic English.
- Avoid unnecessary intensifiers such as "very", "extremely", and "remarkably" unless supported.

For EN to CN:

- Preserve technical terminology.
- Use formal Chinese academic style.
- Avoid colloquial explanation unless requested.

## Output Completeness

- Does the response follow the selected output template?
- Are assumptions or ambiguities clearly stated?
- Are suggested additions separated from revised content?

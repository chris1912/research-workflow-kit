# English Academic Style Guide

## General Principles

English academic writing should be precise, formal, concise, logically connected, evidence-bounded, and field-appropriate.

## Prefer Formal Verbs

Use:

- demonstrate
- indicate
- suggest
- improve
- reduce
- mitigate
- investigate
- evaluate
- validate
- formulate

Avoid informal or promotional expressions:

- perfectly solve
- amazingly improve
- greatly boost without evidence
- prove unless mathematically proven

## Claim Strength

Prefer:

- "The results suggest that..."
- "The proposed method improves ... on the evaluated datasets."
- "This design helps mitigate..."
- "Further validation is required to assess..."

Avoid:

- "The proposed method solves the problem completely."
- "Our method is universally applicable."
- "The method achieves the best performance in all cases."

## Translation Notes

For Chinese-to-English translation:

- Translate meaning, not word order.
- Preserve logical relations.
- Use standard field terminology.
- Avoid overusing "effectively".
- Avoid translating "具有重要意义" as "has important significance" unless the sentence explains why.

## Common Improvements

| Less Academic | More Academic |
|---|---|
| very good results | promising results on the evaluated datasets |
| lots of experiments | extensive experiments |
| solve the problem | address the problem |
| get better features | learn more discriminative features |
| has big value | has potential value |
